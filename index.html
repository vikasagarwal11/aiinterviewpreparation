Episode 1

What Really Happens When You Ask ChatGPT a Question?

Estimated time: 20–30 minutes · Difficulty: Beginner · Interview importance: Very High

Real-Life Story: Driving From Home to Office

You leave home with one goal: reach the office. You do not manually calculate every turn. You rely on GPS, traffic updates, memory, road signs, and real-time decisions. Maybe you stop for coffee. Maybe you take a call. Maybe the GPS reroutes you because of traffic.

That is how modern AI systems work. The user gives a destination. The AI platform figures out the route.

1

User Goal

“Summarize this PDF” or “My child has fever.”

2

Intent Detection

The system decides what the user is trying to do.

3

Context Assembly

Relevant history, files, rules, and system instructions are gathered.

4

Tool / Retrieval Decision

The system decides whether it needs search, database calls, APIs, or uploaded files.

5

Model Reasoning

The LLM generates or reasons over the assembled context.

6

Validation

Outputs may be checked against schemas, rules, safety constraints, or business logic.

7

Response

The answer streams back to the UI.

Technical Translation

A ChatGPT-style system is not “just a model.” It is an application layer around a model: authentication, conversation state, prompt construction, tool routing, retrieval, inference, safety checks, streaming, and UI rendering.

Interview Answer

“When a user sends a message, the platform first interprets intent, assembles relevant context, applies system and safety instructions, optionally retrieves external knowledge or calls tools, sends the constructed prompt to a model, validates the output, and streams the response back.”

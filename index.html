Public-Safety Rule

The public version of Athena should teach the concepts. It should not disclose proprietary architecture, named internal workflows, unpublished product details, exact implementation choices, data schemas, or patent-sensitive ideas.

Safe to publish

Generic AI concepts, interview frameworks, analogies, public examples, and non-proprietary teaching content.

Keep private

Actual system internals, agent decomposition, clinical/safety rules, data schemas, architecture diagrams, and roadmap specifics.

Best protection

Use Render auth, password protection, private repo, or take the service offline. noindex is only a speed bump, not a lock.

The Athena Method

Athena is not a theory-first AI course. It teaches the way a senior builder learns: story first, problem first, judgment first.

01

Story before theory

Start with driving, restaurants, office security, family trips, or building a house. Then map the story to the AI concept.

02

Problem before technology

Ask the real problem first: how do we answer from changing private knowledge without hallucinating?

03

Decision frameworks

Learn when to use RAG, fine-tuning, agents, deterministic workflows, guardrails, and human review — and when not to.

04

Interview altitude

Every concept has a CEO version, engineer version, Principal TPM version, and skeptical follow-up version.

Episode 1

What Really Happens When You Ask ChatGPT a Question?

Estimated time: 20–30 min · Difficulty: Beginner · Interview importance: Very High

Why this matters

Weak candidates say “ChatGPT is an LLM.” Strong candidates explain the full AI application system: intent, context assembly, retrieval, tool routing, inference, validation, observability, cost control, and response streaming.

Real-Life Story: Driving From Home to Office

You leave home with one goal: reach the office. You do not manually calculate every turn — you rely on GPS, traffic updates, memory, road signs, and real-time decisions. Maybe you stop for coffee. Maybe the GPS reroutes you.

That is how modern AI systems work. The user gives a destination. The platform figures out the route.

1

User Goal

“Summarize this PDF” or “Help me understand this issue.”

2

Intent Detection

The system decides what the user is trying to do.

3

Context Assembly

Relevant history, files, rules, and system instructions are gathered.

4

Tool / Retrieval Decision

Does it need search, database calls, APIs, or uploaded files?

5

Model Reasoning

The LLM generates or reasons over the assembled context.

6

Validation

Outputs checked against schemas, rules, safety constraints, and business logic.

7

Response

The answer streams back to the UI.

Technical Translation

A ChatGPT-style system is not just a model. It is an application layer around a model: authentication, conversation state, prompt construction, tool routing, retrieval, inference, safety checks, streaming, and UI rendering.

Interview Answer

When a user sends a message, the platform interprets intent, assembles relevant context, applies system and safety instructions, optionally retrieves knowledge or calls tools, sends the constructed prompt to a model, validates the output, and streams the response back.

Decision Frameworks

Judgment questions that separate Principal from Senior. The interviewer is testing whether you can choose, not whether you memorized a definition.

How Principal Thinking Changes the Question

A junior answer explains the term. A Principal answer explains the decision, risk, trade-off, rollout, and measurement.

Junior lens

“How do embeddings work?”

Senior lens

“Which embedding model, vector database, and retrieval strategy should we use?”

Principal lens

“Should we use embeddings here at all, how will we measure retrieval quality, and what failure mode could hurt the business?”

Program / TPM Layer

Role-specific questions: model upgrades, regression handling, cost governance, vendor strategy, and rollout.
